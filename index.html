What was that?

That was an exercise in Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, or ACT. ACT is an action oriented approach to psychotherapy that stems from traditional behavior therapy and cognitive behavioral therapy.

Learn More

Why ACT?

To put it simply, life is hard. Human beings were never meant to handle the stress and anxiety that comes with life in the modern era. In order to break the norm that is always being in a state of distress, we must learn new techniques to control our thoughts and emotions.

With ACT, we can use excercises like Leaves on the Stream to learn to let go of the attachment and over identification that with thoughts and feelings that cause suffering.
