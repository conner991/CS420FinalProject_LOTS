About Us

Our Mission

Modern society faces many challenges when it comes to maintaining a healthy lifestyle. One important area of health that often gets overlooked is mental health and the psychological work associated with it.
With the hope of increasing awareness of mental health therapies in general, our mission with Leaves on the Stream (LOTS) is to simply house a place that can provide help for anyone and everyone in mental distress.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

The Problem

The main content of LOTS revolves around an approach to psychotherapy called Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT). Steming from the roots of traditional behavioral therapy and cognitive behavioral therapy, ACT teaches the clients to stop avoiding, denying, and struggling with their inner emotions. When we fuse ourselves to our thoughts and emotions, we become less likely to be able to let go of the attachment and over identification to those thoughts. This can cause immense suffering in the form of emotional pain, anxiety, or stress.

The Solution

The key to relieving ones own mental distress -- a key that is baked right into the foundation of ACT -- is to practice accepting that the deeper feelings that arise from troublesome thoughts are merely just appropriate responses to certain situations. ACT helps us understand that our emotions, no matter how problematic, should never stop us from moving forward in our lives.

ACT provides us with a tool to practice letting go or cognitive diffusion. The first step of cognitive diffusion is to recognize that you are the observer of your thoughts and not the thoughts themselves. We must create space in our mind and notice when thoughts arise and learn to not let them take control of our emotions. If one can have the willingness to look at their thoughts in a new way, then one can begin to free themselves from unnecessary emotional suffering.
